WRIGHT PARK

Cool climate, wonderful scenery, and horseback riding adventure — these are just some of the reasons why Wright Park has become one of the most visited and well-loved parks in the Philippines’ summer capital, Baguio City. The park is named after Governor Luke E. Wright who ordered architect Daniel Burnham to develop the place for American soldiers and civilians alike to have an area for recreation and leisure. The Wright Park Riding Circle holds the main activity that people go to the park for — horseback riding. In fact, Wright Park is often mistakenly called “Ride Park” especially by kids because of the riding activity done here.

ADDRESS:

beside Rizal Elementary School, Gibraltar Rd, Baguio, Benguet, Philippines

CAMP JOHN HAY

Camp John Hay, a haven tucked away in the City of Pines, offers you a taste of joy and adventure. Each and every corner is jam-packed with entertainment options, activity hubs, retail stores, and dining establishments. after the pandemic To enter the facility, wellness passes can be secured at the SEZAD Office near IHG. Visitors are reminded to wear their face masks at all times and observe social distancing. Food may not be brought in but drinking water is allowed inside.

ADDRESS:

Loakan Road, Baguio City, Philippines 2600

BOTANICAL GARDEN

If you’re looking for a picturesque and relaxing outdoor escape, look no further than the Botanical Garden in Baguio City. Nestled in the heart of the Philippines’ Cordillera region, this sprawling garden is home to various indigenous plants and flowers. Whether you’re a nature lover or just looking for a beautiful spot to take some Instagram-worthy shots, visiting the Botanical Garden of Baguio City will surely impress!

ADDRESS:

37 Leonard Wood Road, Baguio City

MINES VIEW PARK

Mines View Park is one of the famous tourist spots across Baguio pack with shops selling snacks, trinkets, botanical items, and others. Picture taking services with traditional Igorot clothes, horses, dogs and more are also available towards the lower half of the park. While in the edge of the Mines View Park lies a scenic view of mountains and forest, it also overlooked Itogon’s abandoned mines which are also known as Balatoc Mines. Due to its view, the observation deck of Mines View Park became a popular destination to take photos of Baguio’s scenery. This spot in the park also provides an open hut inclusive of chairs, to give shade and a resting area for visitors.

ADDRESS:

Mines View Observation Deck, Mines View, Baguio, Benguet

BURNHAM PARK

Located along Jose Abad Santos Drive in the heart of Baguio City, Burnham Park offers a refreshing escape from city life. Inside this 32-hectare park, you'll find different clusters that consist of various attractions like a children's playground, a rose garden, an orchidarium, a picnic grove, and a traditional Igorot garden. Its main highlight, however, is a man-made lake in the center of the park. A visit here is not complete without riding a boat across the lake for a relaxing morning or afternoon amongst the trees. If you can, it is recommended to rent one of the swan boats here as well for a more memorable experience.

ADDRESS:

Jose Abad Santos Dr, Baguio, 2600 Benguet

NIGHT MARKET

ADDRESS:

DIPLOMAT HOTEL

Diplomat Hotel is an eerie abandoned structure with a sinister past is rumored to be one of the Philippines' most haunted locations.

ADDRESS:

Dominican Hill, Diplomat Road, Baguio City

THE MANSION

The Mansion is the President of the Philippines' official summer residence. From 1908 through 1935, it served as the summer residence of the Governors-General of the United States.

ADDRESS:

Romulo Drive, Baguio City

BAGUIO CATHEDRAL

Baguio Cathedral is one of the more familiar icons and most visited landmarks in Baguio City. The Cathedral stands tall with its distinctive rose-pink color and twin belfries, which are reminiscent of Malaysia's Petronas Twin Towers.

ADDRESS:

Mount Mary, Cathedral Loop, Baguio City
