Explore the Enchanting North!

Burnham Park

Discover the heart of Baguio City at Burnham Park. Nestled amidst lush greenery, this iconic urban park offers a serene escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Enjoy leisurely strolls around its picturesque lake, rent a paddleboat, or relish a relaxing picnic in its expansive gardens. Burnham Park is the perfect spot to unwind, breathe in the fresh mountain air, and soak in the beauty of Baguio's landscape.

Mines View

Immerse yourself in breathtaking panoramic views of the mining town turned tourist attraction at Mines View Park. Located atop a hill, this spot offers awe-inspiring vistas of the Cordillera Mountains and the surrounding valleys. Capture the moment as you stand beside the observation deck, don traditional Igorot attire, or browse through local crafts and souvenirs. Mines View is a must-visit destination for a glimpse into the region's rich cultural heritage and natural splendor.

Learn more!

Night Market

As the sun sets, Baguio's Night Market comes alive with vibrant energy and a lively atmosphere. Explore the bustling streets filled with a diverse array of stalls offering everything from clothing and accessories to local handicrafts and delectable street food. Engage with friendly vendors, bargain for unique finds, and savor the delectable flavors that define Baguio's culinary scene. The Night Market is a sensory delight that captures the essence of the city's lively spirit.

Things to Remember When Visiting Baguio


Planning Your Trip

Decide on the best time to visit. The weather in Baguio is relatively cooler, making it a great escape from the heat, especially during the summer months from March to May. Book your accommodations in advance, especially if you're visiting during peak tourist seasons.

Getting There

Baguio is accessible by bus from Manila and other major cities. The bus journey takes around 4-7 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

Embrace the Culture

Visit the Tam-awan Village to learn about the traditional Cordilleran way of life and art. Explore local markets like the Baguio Public Market to sample fresh produce, crafts, and street food.

Food and Dining

Baguio is known for its strawberries, so don't miss trying strawberry-flavored treats like jams and taho (a local snack made from silken tofu). Taste local dishes such as strawberry taho, ukoy (shrimp fritters), and ube (purple yam) jam.

Traffic and Transportation

Traffic can get congested in the city center, especially during peak hours, so consider walking or using public transportation to get around.
