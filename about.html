—— Geography

Baguio City, located in the northern Philippines, is nestled in the Cordillera Central mountain range. It sits approximately 1,540 meters (5,050 feet) above sea level, offering a cool and temperate climate. Surrounded by lush pine forests, it is known as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines." The city is characterized by its picturesque landscapes, including the famous Burnham Park and Mines View Park.

—— Culture

Baguio City's culture is a captivating fusion of indigenous traditions and contemporary influences. The city cherishes its diverse cultural heritage, with the Ibalois, Kankanaeys, and other indigenous communities preserving their unique customs and crafts. Baguio's warm hospitality and scenic beauty attract tourists from across the globe while embracing Filipino traditions and values.

See Culture

—— Language

Ilocano stands as the dominant dialect in Baguio City, while Filipino, the national language, is spoken by nearly all residents. English enjoys widespread usage and comprehension, serving as the primary language of instruction in both public and private schools. This linguistic versatility extends to the print media, ensuring that English-speaking visitors face no communication barriers.

See Language

—— Tradition

Baguio City upholds several cherished traditions, including the Panagbenga Festival, a vibrant celebration of flowers and street dancing held annually. The city also embraces indigenous customs and rituals of the Ibaloi, Kankanaey, and other ethnic groups, fostering a deep connection to the region's cultural heritage. Baguio's rich tradition of hospitality and warmth welcomes visitors to experience its unique blend of local and Filipino traditions.

See Tradition

—— History

Baguio City, located in the Philippines, was established as a hill station by the Americans in 1900 during their colonial rule. It served as a retreat from the tropical heat and later became a major city. Baguio played a role in World War II and was partially destroyed. It was rebuilt and has since thrived as a popular tourist destination, known for its cool climate and scenic beauty.

Bignay wine is a sweet, fruity Filipino fruit wine made from the bignay tree's berries, with a deep red color and a fruity aroma.

Bulalo
$8-$20

It is a light colored soup that is made by cooking beef shanks and marrow bones until the collagen and fat has melted into the clear broth.

Longganisa
$5

Baguio longganisa is a sweet Filipino pork sausage from Baguio City, similar to hamonado longganisa.

Pinuneg
$4

Pinuneg is a Filipino blood sausage made with pig's blood, minced pork fat, onions, ginger, garlic, and stuffed into pig's small intestine casing.

Peanut Brittle
$2-$6

Nuts and caramelized sugar are mixed and combined with spices, leavening agents, peanut butter, and butter. The hot candy is then poured onto a flat surface to cool.

Strawberry Taho
